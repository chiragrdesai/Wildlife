TREKKING IN INDIA : DISCOVERING NATURE

Trekking is not merely a mountain walk, rather it is an exploration of every thing that resides within the mountain range. It is a journey beyond the confines of nature and is also a rendezvous with the cultural diaspora that vibrates within the mountaneous range.

Above all, it is a complete and unique experience which satisfies adventurous ambitions and provides inner fulfilment. Most people return from the Himalayas profoundly moved by this experience, but for some of us it completely changes our lives. The influence it exerts becomes irresistible and we find ourselves longing to make that annual pilgrimage to those most cherished mountains.

Types of Trekking
There are 3 types of Trekking programmes.

Standard Trekking
These types are specially tailor-made trekking programme according to the taste of the clients.

Normal Conventional Trekking
These programmes are based upon fixed trekking trails, passing through mountain villages simultaneously experiencing the Nature and near the Himalayas.

Tough Trekking
Tough Treks are of longer duration through rugged trails gradually rising to very high altitudes and some may consist Trekking Peaks around 6000 Meters.

Quality of Trekking
Organized camp trekking :
This will be catered by a whole crew of staff that takes care of the trekkers with utmost care and also provides the best possible facilities and luxuries.

Tea- House Trekking :
A Tea- House Trek means the trekkers are provided lodging facilities in the village guest-houses at night. This is available only in some trekking areas and plays a vital role in bringing culture closer. This is an excellent way to experience the mountaneous trail .

Trekking Grades
Easy
These treks are especially meant for the first timers as well as those traveling with children or seniors. You won't be walking more than 5-6 hours each day and the trails do not exceed 2000 Mts. in height.

Moderate
The trails on these treks do not exceed 4000 Mts. in height. Walk will be somewhere between 6-7 hours a day and can be undertaken by children (10-12 years) and seniors with good physical conditions.

Demanding
Trips operating in remote areas and in altitude more than 5000 Mts. You need to be in very good shape and physically fit. You will have some really arduous days on the trail. Physical preparation beforehand is very necessary.
